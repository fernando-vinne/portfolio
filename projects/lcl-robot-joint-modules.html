Prototype construction and mechanical interface design for a research project focused on low-cost, customizable, and rigid lightweight robotic systems. Overview

This project was part of a research effort to develop a process for the semi-automatic design of low-cost lightweight robots. The linkages were designed using topology optimization, while the overall robot concept aimed to combine advances in AI-assisted design, 3D printing, lightweight robot concepts, and modular actuation.

The work was connected to a joint development context between LPL and the Institute of Micro Technology and Medical Device Technology. The goal was to make low-cost robotic systems more customizable, lightweight, rigid, and suitable for broader use.

My contribution

My role was to design the motor modules and linkage connections so the lightweight robot concept could be physically assembled and studied. A key part of this work was defining practical interface constraints for the topology-optimized linkages.

  • Designed motor modules for the modular lightweight robot concept.
  • Designed linkage connection interfaces for topology-optimized robot links.
  • Helped define which areas had to remain free for assembly, tools, cables, and access.
  • Supported the physical construction of the robot prototype.
  • Contributed to a research project later connected to a publication acknowledgement.

Engineering challenges

Lightweight interfaces

  • The modules had strong weight constraints while still needing to transfer loads reliably.
  • The interfaces had to support topology-optimized linkages without blocking assembly access.
  • Connection points had to be practical for real construction, not only theoretical optimization.

Modular construction

  • The robot joints needed to remain modular and understandable for assembly and testing.
  • Tools, cables, fasteners, and print limitations had to be considered during interface design.
  • The design had to translate research concepts into a physical prototype that could actually be built.

What I learned

This was one of my first deeper experiences with robotic joints, modular robot design, SLA-printed parts, and end-effector/module construction. It also showed me how important interfaces are when optimized parts need to become real assemblies.

The project was especially valuable because it connected mechanical design, lightweight structures, robotic modules, and practical prototype construction in a research context.
